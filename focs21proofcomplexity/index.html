Proof Complexity Workshop

Overview

The area of proof complexity was introduced by Cook and Reckhow in 1979 as a way to tackle the NP vs coNP problem. The overarching goal of the field is to understand how difficult it is to prove certain mathematical facts. Traditionally, the complexity of theorem is measured as the length of the shortest proof of the theorem in a given formal system. Since the early years of proof complexity many techniques have been developed to analyze concrete proof systems, thus making progress towards understanding the reasoning power of increasingly strong systems.

There has been a revival of the field in the last 10-15 years, with many exciting results and new connections to other areas such as, for example, automated proof search, combinatorial optimization, total function problems in NP (TFNP), minimum circuit size problem (MCSP), cryptography, learning, and algebraic complexity. The goal of this workshop is to present some of the classical theory in proof complexity and also survey some of these recent exciting connections.
